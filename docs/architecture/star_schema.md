# Overview

The Enterprise Retail Sales Analytics Data Warehouse follows a Star Schema architecture. A Star Schema is a dimensional modeling technique optimized for analytical reporting and business intelligence workloads.

The design separates measurable business events into Fact Tables and descriptive business entities into Dimension Tables.

This approach provides:

- High query performance
- Simplified reporting
- Easy aggregation
- Better scalability
- Optimized Power BI models

# Fact Tables

## fact_sales

### Business Process

Records every product sold through physical stores and online channels.

### Grain

One record represents one product sold within a single sales transaction.

### Measures

- Quantity Sold
- Unit Price
- Discount Amount
- Sales Amount
- Cost Amount
- Profit Amount

### Foreign Keys

- customer_key
- product_key
- store_key
- employee_key
- promotion_key
- date_key

### Source Systems

- POS
- Online Store

---

## fact_inventory_snapshot

### Business Process

Captures daily inventory levels for products across stores and warehouses.

### Grain

One record per product, per store, per day.

### Measures

- Current Stock
- Reserved Stock
- Available Stock
- Reorder Level

### Foreign Keys

- product_key
- supplier_key
- store_key
- date_key

### Source System

- Inventory

# Surrogate Keys

Every dimension table uses an integer surrogate key generated by the ETL process.

Benefits include:

- Faster joins
- Historical tracking
- SCD Type 2 support
- Stable warehouse identifiers

---

# Slowly Changing Dimensions (SCD)

The warehouse uses **SCD Type 2** for selected dimensions.

Applicable dimensions:

- dim_customer
- dim_product
- dim_store
- dim_employee
- dim_promotion
- dim_supplier

Additional columns include:

- effective_start_date
- effective_end_date
- is_current

This allows historical tracking of attribute changes while preserving reporting accuracy.

---

# Conformed Dimensions

The following dimensions are shared across multiple fact tables:

- dim_date
- dim_product
- dim_supplier
- dim_store

Using conformed dimensions ensures consistent reporting across different business processes.

---

# Indexing Strategy

Indexes will be created on:

- All surrogate keys
- Business keys
- Foreign keys
- Frequently filtered columns
- Frequently joined columns

This improves query performance and reporting speed.

---

# Partitioning Strategy

Future versions of the warehouse may partition the fact tables by transaction date.

Benefits include:

- Faster reporting
- Easier maintenance
- Improved incremental loading performance

---

# Power BI Integration

The Star Schema is optimized for Power BI.

Benefits include:

- Simple relationships
- Fast DAX calculations
- Reduced model complexity
- Better dashboard performance

# Summary

The Star Schema provides a scalable, high-performance analytical model for the Enterprise Retail Sales Analytics Data Warehouse. The design follows dimensional modeling best practices and supports enterprise reporting, historical analysis, incremental ETL, and Slowly Changing Dimensions.
